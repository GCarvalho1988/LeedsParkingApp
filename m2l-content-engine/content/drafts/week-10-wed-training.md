Four weeks out.

The last long ride is done — 140km on Saturday. The plan says it's the final big one before the taper. My body said fine, but ask me again on Monday.

The legs are where they need to be. The uncertainty now isn't physical — it's whether I can maintain focus for 12–14 hours on the day. That's the part you can't really train for. You either find out you can, or you find out you can't.

Manchester to London is a different event from Fred Whitton. Less dramatic, more sustained. More grinding. No single mountain to frame the suffering neatly. Just 200 miles of road, mostly pointing south.

Four weeks. Still moving.
