The plan says 200 miles on 28 June. My legs currently have opinions about 40.

Three weeks into training. Saturday was a 55km ride into the Wharfe Valley — beautiful, relentless, and ended with me eating a Cornish pasty in a car park in Otley with the blank expression of a man reassessing his choices.

Progress is being made. Just slowly.

The thing about training for something like this is that the gap between where you are and where you need to be is both motivating and slightly terrifying. I'm not a natural endurance cyclist. I'm a person who cycles because he lives in Yorkshire and it would be embarrassing not to.

Ten weeks to go. The legs will follow.
