Fred Whitton: done.

112 miles. 4,000m of climbing. 10 hours and change.

Hardknott Pass was exactly as bad as advertised. The gradient hits you like a question you're not prepared for. I walked about 50 metres of it. Not proud, not ashamed. Just accurate.

The rest was stunning and brutal in roughly equal measure. The Lake District does not care about your training plan.

I finished. My legs are currently requesting early retirement, which I've denied.

More importantly: if I can do Fred Whitton, I can do Manchester to London. Not comfortably. Not fast. But I can do it.

Seven weeks to go.

Fundraising link in the first comment — £1,000 for Ambitious about Autism. If Fred Whitton didn't convince you I'm serious, I'm not sure what will.
