Six weeks of training. The body is adapting. The mind is still negotiating.

This week: back-to-back days on the bike for the first time. Saturday 80km, Sunday 55km. Sunday felt like cycling through wet concrete, but I finished. That's apparently the point.

The honest update: I can now do the distances. What I can't quite do is do them consecutively, at pace, over multiple days. Which is essentially what Manchester to London is.

So there's work to do.

Eight weeks to the ride. Fred Whitton in two. Fred Whitton is 112 miles with 4,000m of climbing in the Lake District — my way of finding out whether the training is real or just self-delusion.

Updates to follow either way.
